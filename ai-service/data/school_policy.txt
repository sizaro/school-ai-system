SCHOOL HANDBOOK AND POLICY DOCUMENT

1. SCHOOL ATTENDANCE POLICY

All students are expected to attend school regularly and participate in all scheduled lessons.

A student must maintain an attendance rate of at least 80% in order to qualify to sit final examinations.

Students who miss classes due to illness or other reasons must provide appropriate communication from parents or guardians.

Repeated absence without explanation may result in disciplinary action and a meeting with parents or guardians.

Teachers are responsible for recording attendance every school day and submitting attendance reports to the administration office.


2. SCHOOL FEES POLICY

All students are required to complete payment of school fees according to the agreed payment schedule.

Students with outstanding unpaid fees may be restricted from participating in final examinations until their accounts are cleared.

The finance department maintains student payment records and provides clearance confirmation.

Parents or guardians who have concerns about payments should contact the finance office.

Fee balances, payment history, and clearance status are stored in the school management system.


3. EXAMINATION POLICY

Students must meet academic and administrative requirements before participating in examinations.

Requirements include meeting the minimum attendance percentage and completing required fee payments.

Examinations are prepared and supervised by teachers according to the academic calendar.

Teachers must submit examination marks within the required reporting period.

Final results are released after marks verification and completion of administrative clearance.


4. STUDENT DISCIPLINE POLICY

Students are expected to follow school rules, respect teachers, and maintain good behavior.

The school does not tolerate behavior that negatively affects learning or the safety of other students.

Minor violations may result in warnings or counseling.

Repeated violations may lead to meetings with parents, suspension, or other disciplinary measures according to school procedures.


5. TEACHER RESPONSIBILITIES

Teachers are responsible for preparing lessons, teaching students, recording attendance, and evaluating student performance.

Teachers must submit academic records and examination results accurately and within deadlines.

Teachers should communicate important student concerns to the administration department.


6. PARENT AND GUARDIAN RESPONSIBILITIES

Parents and guardians should monitor student attendance, academic progress, and school obligations.

Parents should respond to communication from the school and participate in meetings when required.

Parents are encouraged to work with teachers and administrators to support student success.


7. SCHOOL ADMINISTRATION POLICY

The administration manages school operations, student records, staff coordination, and policy enforcement.

Student information is stored securely in the school management system.

Access to student information is controlled according to user roles and permissions.
